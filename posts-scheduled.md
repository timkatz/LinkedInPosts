### Common 2026 planning mistakes

**LinkedIn:**

A brand called us in August wanting to migrate to Shopify by October. We can do that timeline, but here's how it works:

Phase 1 (2-3 months): Launch with a base pre-built theme customized to match brand guidelines. Immediately get the benefits of Shopify—faster site speed, better conversion rates, Shop Pay button, easier content management.

Phase 2 (immediately following): Move into CRO optimization phase. Test, improve, refine. Even the base theme performs better than most legacy platforms.

Most brands think "quick migration" means cutting corners. Smart agencies know it means phased approach.

Here are the planning mistakes we're seeing right now:

**Mistake 1: Planning a Q4 2026 migration but not starting until summer.**

Shopify migrations take 3-4 months for mid-market brands. If you want to be stable by October, you need to launch by June at the latest. That means starting conversations now, not in March.

**Mistake 2: Assuming more traffic solves everything.**

Your growth plan: increase traffic 50%. But if your conversion rate is 1.2%, more traffic just means more waste. Fix conversion first, then scale volume. A site converting at 2.4% needs half the traffic to hit the same revenue.

**Mistake 3: Ignoring site speed and mobile UX.**

Your site loads in 4 seconds. Mobile conversion is half of desktop. These aren't minor issues. Every second of load time costs you 7-8% conversion. That mobile gap is leaving 20-30% of potential revenue on the table.

**Mistake 4: Budgeting based on 2025 spending, not Q4 learnings.**

You spent $400k on ads, so you budget $500k. That's just math, not strategy. If Q4 showed traffic up but conversions flat, more ad spend won't fix it. Shift budget to CRO and site optimization.

**Mistake 5: Switching agencies without diagnosing why the last one failed.**

Brand fires agency in November, hires new one in December, frustrated again by March. The agency wasn't the only problem. Unclear scope, no measurable goals, no honest timelines. Fix that first.

These are fixable if you catch them now. That's what we spend December and January talking through with brands—catching these planning mistakes before they become six-month delays.
